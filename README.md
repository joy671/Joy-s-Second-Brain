# FDI vs FII

## 🧠 Core Idea
FDI is long-term investment with control; FII is short-term, market-driven investment.

---

## 📊 Key Differences

| Factor | FDI | FII |
|-------|-----|-----|
| Nature | Long-term | Short-term |
| Control | High | None |
| Stability | Stable | Volatile |
| Impact | Developmental | Market liquidity |

---

## 📊 Indian Context
- FDI in infrastructure, manufacturing
- FII dominates stock market movements

---

## ⚖️ Implications
- FDI builds economy
- FII can destabilize markets

---

## 🧾 Conclusion
Balanced policy needed to attract stable capital while managing volatility.

# Basic Structure Doctrine

## 🧠 Concept
Parliament cannot alter the "basic structure" of the Constitution.

---

## 📜 Origin
Evolved in Kesavananda Bharati case (1973)

---

## ⚖️ Key Elements
- Supremacy of Constitution
- Rule of Law
- Judicial Review
- Separation of Powers

---

## 🧾 Importance
Prevents misuse of amendment powers.

---

## 💡 Insight
Acts as a safeguard against authoritarian tendencies.
